The growing issue surrounding sea levels rising

Article by Lachlan Douglas

Over the last decade the Sea levels have been rising. In 2014 the global sea level was 6.6cm above the 1993 average. At this rate sea level is rising about 3.17cm per year. The rising sea levels are being caused by the thermal expansion of the water as water expands as it gets hotter and melting of the Polar Ice caps which are melting at a faster and faster rate because of global warming. One of the main causes of Global warming and the melting of the polar icecaps is Excess Greenhouse gas emission from factories, cars and ships. As a human race we need greenhouse gases in out atmosphere as without them the earth would be uninhabitable and freezing, however with excess greenhouse gas emissions are causing an increase in global temperatures by keeping the heat from the sun in the atmosphere. Causing the polar icecaps to melt.
