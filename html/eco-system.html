The Effect on the eco-system

Article by Thomas Prior

The effect on the eco-system are severe. Believe or note the images we see of polar bears floating on ice bergs whilst they might be a touch radical it’s not the worst of it. Inuit’s are also seeing a hard time due to there hunting season being reduced making it harder to support themselves. The melting of the ice caps led to a sudden explosion of anthrax in Syria back in 2016. This was the cause of over 2000 reindeer getting infected and dozens of people being hospitalized. The local ecosystems are also heavily affected. Due to the melting icecaps there are more nutrients and with the sufficient light this causes plankton to thrive. Due to plankton being an essential part of an eco-system it means its changing and who knows at this stage if its for the better. The melting artic sea ice is posing a massive threat to seals polar bears and other indigenous species. The sea ice provides a platform for polar bears to hunt on and links communities of land animals. As the sea ice cap recedes it forces the animals on it to move north and there’s only so much moving north that animals can tolerate. These are only some of the effects on the eco-system, but all are severe.
